Founded in 1919, HopeForChildren has been on the front lines of the world's most challenging crises for over 100 years. We've evolved from a small relief organization to a global movement that reaches millions of children each year.

Our approach combines direct program implementation with advocacy and partnerships to create lasting change for children. We work closely with communities, governments, and other organizations to ensure children's rights are protected and their needs are met.

About Us

For over 100 years, we've been fighting for children's rights and saving children's lives.

Our Story

HopeForChildren was founded in 1919 by Eglantyne Jebb in response to the devastating impact of World War I on children. Her revolutionary idea was that children have rights, regardless of who they are or where they're from. This vision led to the creation of the first Declaration of the Rights of the Child, which later inspired the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Today, we work in over 80 countries, including the United States, reaching millions of children each year through our programs in health, nutrition, education, protection and child rights. We're committed to ensuring that every child has the opportunity to grow up healthy, educated and safe.

Our work is guided by our Global Strategy, which focuses on three breakthroughs for children by 2030: Survive, Learn, and Be Protected. We believe that together with our partners, donors, and supporters, we can achieve these ambitious goals.

Health & Nutrition

Ensuring children survive and thrive through access to quality healthcare and proper nutrition.

Saving Children's Lives

Every day, nearly 15,000 children under the age of five die from preventable causes. We're working to change that by providing life-saving healthcare and nutrition to children in the world's poorest communities.

Our health programs focus on maternal and child health, immunization, treatment of common childhood illnesses, and strengthening health systems. We train health workers, equip clinics, and work with communities to promote healthy practices.

Our nutrition programs address both immediate and underlying causes of malnutrition. We provide therapeutic feeding for severely malnourished children, support breastfeeding, promote diverse diets, and address food security issues.

Our Approach

Community Health

We train and support community health workers to provide basic health services, health education, and referrals in hard-to-reach areas where formal health facilities are scarce.

Emergency Health Response

In crises, we deploy emergency health units to provide immediate medical care, prevent disease outbreaks, and support local health systems to continue functioning.

Nutrition Programs

We screen children for malnutrition, provide therapeutic feeding, support infant and young child feeding practices, and address the underlying causes of malnutrition.

Health System Strengthening

We work with governments to strengthen health systems, improve supply chains for essential medicines, and build the capacity of health workers.

Child Protection

Keeping children safe from harm, abuse, exploitation, and violence.

Protecting Children's Rights

Every child has the right to grow up safe from harm. Yet millions of children worldwide experience violence, exploitation, and abuse. We work to prevent and respond to these violations of children's rights.

Our child protection programs focus on preventing and responding to violence, exploitation, and abuse. We work with children, families, communities, and governments to create protective environments where children can thrive.

In emergencies, we establish child-friendly spaces where children can be safe, play, and receive psychosocial support. We also work to reunite separated children with their families and provide alternative care when needed.

Our Protection Work

Child-Friendly Spaces

In emergencies, we establish safe spaces where children can play, learn, and receive psychosocial support. These spaces protect children from harm and help them cope with trauma.

Family Tracing and Reunification

When children are separated from their families in emergencies, we work to trace and reunite them with their relatives as quickly as possible.

Community-Based Protection

We work with communities to establish child protection committees and systems that identify, prevent, and respond to child protection risks.

Advocacy for Child Rights

We advocate for laws, policies, and practices that protect children from violence, exploitation, and abuse, and promote their rights and well-being.

Our Mission

To inspire breakthroughs in the way the world treats children, and to achieve immediate and lasting change in their lives.

Our Vision

We believe every child deserves a future. Since our founding in 1919, we've changed the lives of over 1 billion children.

We work in the hardest-to-reach places, where it's toughest to be a child. We do whatever it takes to ensure children survive, get protection when they're in danger, and have the chance to learn.

Our work is grounded in the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child, and we campaign tirelessly to make children's rights a reality.

About Us

For over 100 years, we've been fighting for children's rights and saving children's lives.

Our History

Founded in 1919 by Eglantyne Jebb in response to the plight of children affected by World War I, we've grown to become the world's leading independent organization for children.

Our founder drafted the first Declaration of the Rights of the Child, which was adopted by the League of Nations in 1924 and later formed the basis of the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child.

Today, we work in over 100 countries, reaching millions of children each year through our programs in health, nutrition, education, protection and child rights.

Health & Nutrition

Ensuring children survive and thrive with access to quality healthcare and proper nutrition.

Saving Children's Lives

Every day, 15,000 children under 5 die from preventable causes. We're working to change that by providing life-saving healthcare and nutrition to children in the world's poorest communities.

Our health programs focus on maternal and child health, immunization, treatment of common childhood illnesses, and prevention of malnutrition.

We train health workers, strengthen health systems, and ensure communities have access to the healthcare they need.

Support Our Work

Our Health Programs

MATERNAL & CHILD HEALTH

We work to ensure mothers and babies survive childbirth and the first critical years of life through prenatal care, skilled birth attendance, postnatal care, and immunization services.

NUTRITION

We treat and prevent malnutrition through community-based management of acute malnutrition, promoting breastfeeding, and providing supplementary feeding for vulnerable children.

COMMUNITY HEALTH

We train and support community health workers to provide basic health services, health education, and referrals in remote and underserved areas.

EMERGENCY HEALTH RESPONSE

In crises, we deploy mobile health clinics, provide emergency medical supplies, and support health facilities to ensure continuity of care.

Child Protection

Keeping children safe from harm, abuse, exploitation, and violence.

Protecting Children from Harm

Every child has the right to be safe from violence, exploitation, and abuse. Yet millions of children worldwide face these threats every day.

Our child protection programs work to prevent and respond to violence against children, including physical and humiliating punishment, sexual abuse, child marriage, child labor, and trafficking.

We establish child-friendly spaces in emergencies, support child protection systems, and work with communities to change harmful practices.

Support Child Protection

Our Protection Work

CHILD-FRIENDLY SPACES

In emergencies, we establish safe spaces where children can play, learn, and receive psychosocial support, protected from harm.

FAMILY TRACING AND REUNIFICATION

When children are separated from their families in crises, we work to find them and reunite them with their caregivers.

ENDING VIOLENCE AGAINST CHILDREN

We work to eliminate physical punishment, bullying, and other forms of violence against children in all settings.

CHILD PARTICIPATION

We ensure children have a voice in decisions that affect them and can advocate for their own rights and protection.

Modern Slavery Statement

Our commitment to preventing modern slavery and human trafficking

The 2015 Modern Slavery Act requires large UK organisations to publish annual statements to report on the steps they are taking to ensure that modern slavery and human trafficking are not taking place in their operations and in the supply chains that provide their goods and services in the respective financial year.

Each year, the Hope For Kids International Board Statement sets out the progress which has been made since previous year's Statement. In the course of preparing the statement, Hope For Kids International have considered practical actions which we have taken and are taking to achieve the commitments detailed by the Board last year, and to move closer to preventing, detecting and responding to issues of modern slavery in our organisation and supply chains. In addition to acting on the specific commitments, additional work has been undertaken to combat this issue across the organisation.
